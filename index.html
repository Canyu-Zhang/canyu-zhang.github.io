I am a junior at NYU Shanghai studying Computer Science (GPA: 3.91/4.0). My research focuses on efficient AI systems — specifically on making large language models faster and more capable under real-world constraints such as long-context inference, retrieval-augmented generation, and memory efficiency.

I have worked on KV cache recomputation (InfoFlow KV), submodular context compression (Sub-CP), and benchmarking deep research agents (SAGE). I am broadly interested in:

  • Efficient LLM inference — KV cache management, attention approximation, speculative decoding
  • Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) — context selection, dense retrieval, test-time scaling
  • Embodied AI & Robotics — I am excited about connecting efficient perception and reasoning to physical systems, particularly robot manipulation and navigation grounded in vision-language models

selected publications

  1. ICML
    InfoFlow KV: Information-Flow-Aware KV Recomputation for Long Context
    Canyu Zhang, Shengjie Wang, Tianyi Zhou, and 1 more author
    In International Conference on Machine Learning (ICML 2026), 2026
    *Co-first author
    Abs Bib

    We propose InfoFlow KV, a chunk-based KV prefilling and recomputation pipeline enabling reusable KV caches and recovering cross-chunk attention under long-context inference across multiple LLMs. We introduce two information-flow-aware recomputation strategies: a query-conditioned attention-norm token selection method and a chunk reordering scheme that improves prompt-context interaction, achieving +6% F1 on HotpotQA over prior methods.

  2. preprint
    Submodular Context Partitioning and Compression for In-Context Learning
    Canyu Zhang, Shengjie Wang, and Tianyi Zhou
    2025
    Abs Bib

    We study Sub-CP, a block-aware context selection framework for in-context learning that leverages submodular objectives to balance global diversity and local coherence across demonstration context blocks. We achieve up to +7.1% average F1 score over baseline partition methods and +15.4% F1 score on TREC.

  3. ACL
    SAGE: Benchmarking and Improving Retrieval for Deep Research Agents
    Canyu Zhang and Chen Zhao
    In Annual Meeting of the Association for Computational Linguistics (ACL 2026), 2026
    Abs Bib

    We build SAGE, a benchmark for reasoning-intensive scientific literature retrieval with 1,200 queries across four domains and a 200K-paper corpus. We evaluate six deep research agents under both web search and corpus retrieval settings, and propose a corpus-level test-time scaling framework that improves retrieval accuracy by +8% (short-form) and +2% (open-ended).

  4. PhysRev
    Can Quantum-Mechanical Description of Physical Reality Be Considered Complete?
    A. Einstein*†, B. Podolsky*, and N. Rosen*
    Phys. Rev., New Jersey. More Information can be found here , May 1935
    Abs DOI HTML PDF Video

    In a complete theory there is an element corresponding to each element of reality. A sufficient condition for the reality of a physical quantity is the possibility of predicting it with certainty, without disturbing the system. In quantum mechanics in the case of two physical quantities described by non-commuting operators, the knowledge of one precludes the knowledge of the other. Then either (1) the description of reality given by the wave function in quantum mechanics is not complete or (2) these two quantities cannot have simultaneous reality. Consideration of the problem of making predictions concerning a system on the basis of measurements made on another system that had previously interacted with it leads to the result that if (1) is false then (2) is also false. One is thus led to conclude that the description of reality as given by a wave function is not complete.
